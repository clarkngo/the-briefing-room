Designing Agency: A Framework for AI-Powered Coding Assistants in Higher Education

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE PEDAGOGY HUMAN-COMPUTER INTERACTION
Executive Summary: This research explores the shift from AI as a "tool" to AI as an "agent" within computer science curricula. It proposes a framework for ensuring students maintain cognitive agency while leveraging large language models (LLMs) for software development.

Problem Statement

As AI coding assistants become ubiquitous, the risk of "cognitive offloading" in higher education increases. How do we design interfaces that encourage learning rather than just completion?

Proposed Framework

The "Agency-First" framework focuses on three pillars:

  • Intervention Timing: When should the AI suggest a solution?
  • Explanatory Depth: Requiring the AI to justify logic before providing code.
  • Verification Loops: Student-led testing of AI-generated snippets.

Full manuscript currently under review for The Journal of Multidisciplinary Research and Scholarship.
