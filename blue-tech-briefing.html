Strategic Partnership: City University of Seattle & Washington Maritime Blue

The Proposal: Academic Membership

By joining Washington Maritime Blue as a non-profit academic member, CityU STC secures a leadership position in the Pacific Northwest's emerging "Blue Economy."

Why this matters: The Maritime Innovation Center (MInC) is a partnership between Maritime Blue and the Port of Seattle. Membership provides CityU direct access to this $30B cluster, connecting our students to over 150 industry leaders including Microsoft, Amazon, and global shipping titans.

Why CityU Should Join (Membership Benefits)

  • Joint Innovation Projects (JIPs): Faculty and students can participate in high-stakes R&D for maritime 5G, autonomous navigation, and "Quiet Sound" AI initiatives.
  • Industry-Aligned Curriculum: Direct input from the Maritime Blue cluster to ensure STC's AI and Cybersecurity programs meet 2026 industry standards.
  • Workforce Development Grants: Access to state and federal funding specifically earmarked for "Blue Economy" career training.
  • Direct Career Pipeline: Priority access to the "Blue Venture Accelerator" startups for student placement and capstone projects.

7 Strategic Actions for STC

  1. Formalize Membership: Secure non-profit academic status within the Maritime Blue cluster.
  2. Physical AI Integration: Update AI curriculum to include "Edge Computing" for maritime sensors.
  3. "Living Lab" Access: Schedule a site visit to the MInC for STC faculty to identify lab-sharing opportunities.
  4. JIP Participation: Volunteer STC as the "AI Research Lead" for upcoming 2026 Joint Innovation Projects.
  5. Founder Mentorship: Connect STC students with CEOs from the Blue Venture Accelerator for 1-on-1 coaching.
  6. Cyber-Maritime Focus: Develop a niche specialization in securing maritime autonomous systems (a massive industry gap).
  7. Regional Branding: Market CityU as the premier "Maritime-to-Cloud" university in the PNW.
