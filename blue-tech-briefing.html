Blue Tech & Physical AI Briefing

Strategic Integration Plan for the School of Technology and Computing (STC)

The Opportunity

As of 2026, Seattle has transitioned from a "Software City" to a global leader in Physical AI. For STC, this represents a shift in the labor market: the industry no longer needs just "coders," it needs AI Systems Architects who can deploy intelligence into the maritime environment (Blue Tech).

Physical AI: AI that interacts with the real world (Robotics, Sensors, Edge Computing).
Blue Tech: The $30B+ sustainable maritime economy powered by advanced technology.

7 Strategic Actions for STC

1
Curriculum Pivot: Integrate "Edge AI" and "Sensor Fusion" into the AI core to meet Physical AI demands.
2
Research Grants: Partner with Maritime Blue on the 2026 WA Dept of Commerce Blue Economy calls.
3
"First-Look" Pipelines: Formalize internship agreements with the 21 startups at AI House.
4
Satellite Labs: Use the MInC Fabrication Labs as a second site for STC hardware research.
5
Capstones: Align Senior Capstones with real maritime problems (e.g., Orca protection via AI acoustics).
6
Workforce Literacy: Launch an "AI for Maritime" certificate for traditional industry professionals.
7
Regional Leadership: Brand STC as the "Maritime-to-Cloud" authority of the Pacific Northwest.
